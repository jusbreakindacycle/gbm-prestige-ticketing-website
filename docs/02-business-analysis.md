## 2. Current Digital Presence

The business currently relies on a **Facebook Page** and **direct messaging platforms** (Messenger, Viber, WhatsApp, Telegram, plus phone calls). *(Verified)*

### Strengths
- **Low barrier to entry:** Customers already familiar with Facebook/Messenger can reach the business easily without learning a new platform.
- **Personal, human-driven service:** Direct chat allows for a personalized back-and-forth that a fully automated system might lack.
- **Multi-channel flexibility:** Offering 4 messaging apps plus phone calls lets customers choose their preferred platform.
- **Low operating cost:** No hosting, development, or licensing costs for the current setup.

### Weaknesses
- **Platform dependency risk:** The business's entire public presence depends on Facebook's algorithm, policies, and uptime. A page restriction, algorithm change, or outage directly affects visibility and customer access — the business does not own this channel.
- **Poor discoverability outside the platform:** Facebook Pages are not always well-indexed by general web search engines, so people searching broadly (e.g., "ticketing agency near me" on Google) may not find the business easily.
- **No centralized information:** Service details are likely scattered across multiple Facebook posts over time rather than organized in one reference place, making it harder for new visitors to get a full picture.
- **Repetitive manual inquiries:** Without a central FAQ/services page, staff likely re-answer the same basic questions (what services are offered, how "8-in-1" works, how to reach them) for every new customer.
- **Limited credibility signals:** A Facebook Page alone can look less "official" to some customers compared to a dedicated website, especially for services involving money and legal documents (visa/passport, bills payment).
- **No after-hours self-service information:** If the page admin is offline, a new visitor has no way to learn about services or credibility markers without waiting for a reply.

*(These strengths/weaknesses are reasoned from the general nature of Facebook-based small business operations combined with your description of this business's setup — they are analytical, not measured from actual page metrics, which were not provided.)*

## 4. Current Business Process (AS-IS)

Based on the verified channels available (Facebook, Messenger, Viber, WhatsApp, Telegram, phone), the current process is inferred as follows. **[Assumption — the exact flow should be confirmed with the owner, as this is a reasonable reconstruction, not a confirmed workflow.]**

1. **Discovery:** Customer finds the business through Facebook (page discovery, shares, referrals, or existing follows).
2. **Initial inquiry:** Customer sends a message via Messenger/Viber/WhatsApp/Telegram, or calls directly, asking about a specific service (e.g., "Magkano po ang ticket papuntang Cebu?").
3. **Clarifying questions:** Staff/owner asks for trip details, dates, destination, or document requirements via chat.
4. **Quotation:** Staff manually computes and sends a price quote or requirement checklist through the same chat thread.
5. **Confirmation:** Customer confirms they want to proceed, likely followed by a manual payment arrangement (e.g., bank transfer, e-wallet, cash) outside the scope of this website.
6. **Processing:** Owner/staff processes the actual ticket, booking, or document request with the relevant partner or agency.
7. **Follow-up communication:** Updates and confirmations are sent back through the same chat thread.

### Manual Processes and Inefficiencies Identified
- Every inquiry starts from zero — no persistent, searchable reference exists for the customer to check first.
- Basic service questions ("What is 8-in-1?", "Do you do visa processing?") likely repeat across many different chat threads.
- No single consistent source of business information — details may vary slightly depending on which staff member/channel answers.
- No clear, guided "next step" for a new visitor; they must know to message first before knowing what to ask.
- Information about the business (legitimacy, description, service scope) is not separated from ongoing transactional chat, making it harder to reference later.

## 6. Proposed Business Improvement (TO-BE)

With v1 as a **static, informational website only** (no booking, no payment), the improvements are all about **presentation, clarity, and inquiry generation** — not transaction processing.

- **Online credibility:** A dedicated website with a clear business description, service breakdown, and consistent branding gives the business an independent, professional identity beyond Facebook.
- **Customer experience:** Visitors get an organized, self-paced way to learn about the business before ever needing to send a message — reducing the "cold start" of a first inquiry.
- **Inquiry generation:** Clear calls-to-action ("Message us on Messenger," "Chat via Viber," "Call now") on every service section turn interested visitors into actual inquiries, placed exactly where interest is highest (right after reading about a service).
- **Service presentation:** Each of the 8 services gets its own clear explanation, replacing scattered Facebook posts with one authoritative reference.
- **Business accessibility:** Multiple contact options are presented together in one place, letting customers choose their preferred channel without hunting for it.
- **Brand professionalism:** Consistent design, business information, and messaging tone builds a more "official" impression that can matter for services touching money and government documents.

**Important boundary to maintain:** The website's role stops at **generating and directing inquiries**. It explicitly does **not** perform bookings, accept payments, or show real-time availability — all actual transactions continue exactly as they do now, through phone, Messenger, Viber, WhatsApp, or Telegram.

## 8. Business Constraints

- **Zero-budget development:** No funds allocated for paid hosting, premium templates, custom domains (unless a free option is used), or third-party paid tools. The site must run on free static hosting (e.g., GitHub Pages) and be built without paid software.
- **Limited digital infrastructure:** No existing servers, databases, or backend systems — v1 must work as a fully static site.
- **Reliance on manual customer communication:** All actual transactions still depend on human staff manually replying through chat/phone; the website cannot reduce this dependency in v1.
- **Dependence on third-party travel providers:** The business's ability to fulfill services (tickets, hotel bookings) depends on external airlines, ferry lines, bus companies, and hotels — the website must avoid implying direct control over these (e.g., no promises of real-time pricing or availability).
- **Government process variability (visa/passport):** Requirements and procedures for visa/passport assistance can change over time and vary by destination/agency — the site should describe the service in general terms rather than listing specific, time-sensitive requirements that could go outdated.
